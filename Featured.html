    Polar Vantage M Multisport Watch

    $380

    Rating-

    The slim, lightweight Polar Vantage M is a multisport GPS watch that helps you improve in your favorites sports with 130-plus sports profiles. For weightlifting, the Training Load Pro gives a comprehensive summary of how the activity strains the body by session and over time. It separates cardio load, muscle load, and perceived load. Get your stats on the always-on colorful display.

    The Polar Vantage M works with the training app Polar Flow to analyze progress on your smartphone or computer. The Polar community lets you share and compare progress with other athletes via social media. Additionally, the Polar precision Prime has accurate wrist-based heart rate monitoring and analyzes quality and duration of sleep.

    For runners, the Polar Vantage M features a personalized, adaptable running plan and calculates a running index score after every run. Innovative swim tracking for swimmers detects distance, pace, stroke, swimming style, and rest times. It has a battery life of 30 hours in GPS mode.

    Specifications

    • 130+ sports profiles

    • uses training app Polar Flow

    • Innovative swim tracking
    • GPS connection
    • 30-hour battery life

    Garmin Forerunner 935

    $400

    Rating-

    Serious athletes wanting all the metrics and features they need won't mind the higher price of the Garmin Forerunner 935. And at just .49 grams, weightlifters will appreciate the lightweight, smaller size. Measure steps, pace, gain, training effect, and more with workout alerts. Five buttons around the bezel of fiber-reinforced material give it ease in use.

    The Garmin has an optical heart rate monitor and also connects to an external heart rate strap. For swimmers, it features SWOLF, a score for measuring swimming efficiency in the pool or in open water. Walkers and hikers can make their own routes or follow others by uploading GPX files. A barometric altimeter records elevation gains and losses.

    Smartphone compatible, you get notifications and music controls but without internal music storage. The battery lasts up to two weeks in watch mode, 24 hours in GPS mode, and 50 hours in UltraTrac mode.

    Specifications

    • multisports modes
    • small and lightweight
    • gives SWOLF score
    • uploads GPX files
    • smartphone compatible
    • barometric altimeter
    • battery life of 2 weeks
